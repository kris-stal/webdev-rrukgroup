Environmental Responsibility & Sustainability Policy

In the undertaking of our business operations, we are fully committed to responsible environmental management. Reports suggest that the construction Industry accounts for approximately 50% of global energy usage, 50% of global water usage and 50% of the total usage of raw materials (The Impacts of Construction and the Built Environment 2010, Willmott Dixon).

Maximising efficiency and reducing environmental impacts is a fundamental objective of our business and we have committed to;

  1. Setting objectives which encourage environmental responsibility and sustainability through the reduction of waste.
  2. Monitoring and recording the environmental impact of our business operations.
  3. Ensuring that we comply with all relevant environmental legislation.
  4. Promoting cultural sustainability.

As a responsible contractor, we will provide an appropriate level of resource to ensure that these business principles are maintained and that our progress is regularly communicated. In addition to the above we also;

  1. Encourage reduced dependency on single occupancy car travel.
  2. Encourage the reduction of our environmental impact through sustainable procurement planning.
  3. Encourage a reduction in the consumption of water in our operations.
  4. Encourage our staff to promote the importance of environmental responsibility and sustainability.

This statement has been formally implemented to communicate the importance of environmental responsibility and sustainability in our business operations.

Anti Bribery & Corruption Policy

We prohibit the offer, gift, or acceptance of a bribe in any form, including, but not limited to, kickbacks on any portion of a contract payment or improper benefits made available to clients, sub-contractors, the supply chain or employees of any party. We oppose all forms of corruption, large as well as small, whether in the public or private sector, originating from the UK or abroad.

In order to manage these principles, the following ‘6 Point Plan’ has been adopted;

  1. A commitment from the company directors to ensure the above principles are maintained and to ensure appropriate measures are in place to both detect and prevent bribery and corruption.
  2. A yearly assessment of the risk to bribery and corruption throughout the business and the review/modification of effective and transparent controls to reduce any identifiable exposure.
  3. Establishment of a clear and visible corporate policy which is communicated to all employees.
  4. Periodic reviews to oversee the implementation of this policy and to monitor compliance.
  5. Periodic audits of the policy to ensure effective implementation and awareness is maintained.
  6. This policy has been formally adopted to establish a working environment in which bribery and corruption are never acceptable. Failure to comply with these principles may result in disciplinary action or sanctions/legal proceedings against external parties.

7. Sub-Contractor Rights

You have the following rights:

To ask us to correct any information we hold about you if it is incorrect.

Whilst we endeavour at all times to keep your information accurate, we welcome your corrections. You can correct your profile at any time by contacting us by email and asking us to make any changes. We may ask you for additional verification information if you are changing certain details such as tax reference numbers.

To ask us to erase your information if we no longer have any reason to hold it, also known as the ‘right to be forgotten’.

Our Data Retention Policy (see section 6) explains the circumstances when we can or are obliged to retain information, however outside of those periods we will delete your information in line with our data retention policy and on request. We will maintain a record that you made an erasure request to reduce the likelihood of us contacting you in the future, but we will use that information for no other purpose.

To ask us to return to you information you provided to us, also known as ‘data portability’.

You can ask us to send you in electronic format the information you provided to us under our Terms and Conditions or under consent.

To ask us not to process your information where you previously gave consent or where we are exercising our legitimate interest.

If you make a request for us to stop processing your information, we will investigate to see if there is a compelling reason for processing to continue and will discuss the conclusion of the investigation with you.

You cannot object to processing which is a legal obligation or where we must process your information to satisfy a contract to which you are a party. If you previously gave consent and we processed your data on the basis of that consent, you cannot object to that past processing, however you can ask us to stop processing it in the future.

To ask for a copy of the information we hold about you.

We will endeavour to respond to your request within 30 days, however at time of high demand we may need 90 days to compile a full response.

To ask us not to process your information for marketing purposes.

You can do this by checking or unchecking certain boxes on the forms we use to collect your information.

You may exercise any of the above rights by contacting us at DW@RRUKGROUP.CO.UK or by calling your site contact for R&R Group.
